Skrillex Discography

Click on Album Art to open in iTunes or other store

Note - Not all songs are listed, there are too many unreleased songs!

Extended Plays [EP]


Bells 2008

Bells is Sonny Moore's first album, though it was never released because Atlantic Records pulled its release.

Skrillex Wiki

Gypsyhook EP April, 2009

The first solo release under his real name, Sonny Moore.


Skrillex Wiki Wikipedia


My Name Is Skrillex EP June, 2010

The first album released under Skrillex.


Skrillex Wiki Wikipedia


Scary Monsters and Nice Sprites October, 2010

Second EP by Skrillex. It was originally only released on Beatport, but later made it to CD and other forms.


Skrillex Wiki Wikipedia


More Monsters and Sprites June, 2011

Album continuing Scary Monsters and Nice Sprites. It was originally only released on Beatport, but later made it to CD and other forms.


Skrillex Wiki Wikipedia


Bangarang December, 2011

Bangarang was released on Beatport on December 23rd, 2011, two days before Christmas. The Doors were featured in the song "Right on Time", and Skrillex said in an interview that they were his role models when he was a kid.

Skrillex Wiki Wikipedia


Narcissistic Cannibal: The Remixes February, 2012

This is a song remixed from Korn's tenth studio album, "The Path of Totality".

Wikipedia


Lick It (Remixes) May, 2012

This album has songs remixing Kaskade's "Lick It".



iTunes


After Hours EP August, 2012

This album contains the song "Make it Bun Dem" featuring Damian "Jr. Gong" Marley, Bob Marley's son. This originally had the lyrics to Damian's song "Welcome to Jamrock", but was re-recorded with new vocals.

Skrillex Wiki Wikipedia


Leaving EP February, 2012

Skrillex's 7th EP, was released exclusively to OWSLA's supscription service, The Nest, and then YouTube.

Wikipedia


Try it Out October, 2013

This song was first played in October 2011. A 2012 version is used in Call of Duty: Black Ops II, and the final version (Neon Mix) is a combination of Try it Out and Alvin Risk's "Yeah".

Wikipedia


Recess Remixes July, 2014

This was released as a set of remixes of "Recess", Skrillex's first studio album.

Wikipedia


Ease My Mind v Ragga Bomb Remixes November, 2014

This album has remixes of the songs "Ease My Mind", and "Ragga Bomb". Both are from Recess.

Wikipedia


Dirty Vibe Remixes December, 2014

Dirty Vibe Remixes has remixes of the 6th song on Recess, "Dirty Vibe".

Wikipedia

Studio Albums


Recess March, 2014

Recess is Skrillex's first studio album. Rolling Stone rated it #22 on their 50 Best Albums of 2014 list, and was recorded between 2011 and 2014, when he was touring.

Wikipedia
